Lovely Runner (2024)

You seem to be in the mood for a rom-com. "Lovely Runner" is a K-drama that has taken South Korea (and other parts of the world) by storm in 2024, and you may like it as well if you haven't seen it already.

"Lovely Runner" follows the love story between a young woman named Sol and her favorite singer, Sunjae. After discovering Sunjae's unfortunate fate, Sol miraculously gains the ability to travel back in time and rewrite his future.

While the show may start off with what appears to be a myriad of cliches between a celebrity and a fan, the second episode reveals a twist that may make you more curious than ever about how their love story will play out.

Strangers From Hell (2019)

It seems like you're in the mood for something heavy, so consider tuning into "Strangers from Hell" if you haven't already.

This psychological thriller follows Jongwoo, a young man who moves from a small town to a big city after getting a new job. He moves into a cheap apartment with a group of mysterious residents but comes to learn that the people living there are not so friendly.

"Strangers from Hell" is a title often mentioned in the realm of "dark" K-dramas, with a devoted following. If you like a show that doesn't spell everything out for you, "Strangers from Hell" is a work rich with mysteries.

The show's imagery can be unsettling, so view it at your own risk.

If you want to watch something that makes you feel at home, “Reply 1988” might be a K-drama to add to your watch list. It has a little bit of everything, from comedy to romance. (Though it's more comedic than romantic.)

The lighthearted banter between the five main characters may leave you crying from laughter, while the heartfelt moments between them and their families may overcome you with emotion.

This 2015 K-drama has become a must-watch among K-drama lovers for its simple yet memorable plot, but you might know that if you've seen it already.

You seem to prefer a show with some angst and action, so consider watching "My Name" if you haven't already.

What is a "K-drama"?

"K-drama" is a colloquial term for "Korean drama," which refers to television shows made in South Korea. Koreans use the English loanword "drama" when referring to TV shows, which was included in the term used globally today.

The flag of South Korea. (Image source: Lumia1234, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons)

When did K-dramas become a thing?

The first K-drama dates back to the 1950s, according to the Korean Culture and Information Service. The rest of Asia and the world began to recognize K-dramas with greater technological advancements and easier communication. Increased licensing and streaming services have also made it easier for people worldwide to gain access to K-dramas today.

References:

Yun, S. (2020). Hallyu-binger of Success. Korean Culture and Information Service.

Ju, H. (2018). The Korean Wave and Korean Dramas. Oxford Research Encyclopedia of Communication.

The Korean Broadcasting System (KBS) building in Daejeon, South Korea. KBS launched the country's first ever broadcasting service, which televised the first K-dramas. (Image source: Trainholic, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons)

Are all K-dramas romantic?

No. K-dramas have many genres. Some other popular genres include historical dramas, fantasy dramas, medical dramas, legal dramas, and action dramas. The more popular ones happen to be romantic, and some shows incorporate a romance element regardless of the genre.

A poster of "My Dearest," a 2023 historical drama. Historical dramas are especially loved by K-drama enthusiasts for the traditional attire worn by characters, traditional speech, and scenery reminiscent of dynastic times. (Image source: Korea MBC TV, CC BY-SA 4.0, via Wikimedia Commons)

What is special about K-dramas?

Everyone has their own reasons for what they find appealing about K-dramas, but the melodramatic aspect of them has been a common reason. According to Steve Choe, an associate professor at Georgetown University, the emphasis of simple and complex feelings complemented by the carefully crafted cinematography has been an alluring feature of many K-dramas.

Reference: Choe, S. (2024). Sympathy for the K-Drama. Georgetown Journal of International Affairs.
